Eye Oculus

Overview

User-centered design of a handheld assistive device, iterated from user feedback. The design emphasizes ergonomics, simple interaction and modular electronics for easy maintenance.

Summary

The Eye Oculus project aimed to provide a low-cost assistive device for people with visual impairments. Responsibilities included concept sketches, user testing sessions, and prototype iterations based on feedback. The final prototype was a handheld device with tactile feedback from three small vibration motors based on the sensor readings from three LIDAR sensors facing forward, up and down. The design was completely wireless including a charging port and on/off button. The sensing and actuation logic was implemented in an Arduino Nano microcontroller and the device was fully functional.

  • Role: Concept designer & user testing
  • Methods: interviews, low-fi prototypes, iterative testing
  • Outcome: improved usability and reduced production cost estimate
