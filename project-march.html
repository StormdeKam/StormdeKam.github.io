Project MARCH

Summary

The goal of Project MARCH is to allow a paraplegic to stand up and walk again by designing, building and controlling a powered exoskeleton. My responsibility was the design and production of the motorized joints including ideation, conceptualization and finalization using CAD, tolerance analysis, prototyping and verification testing.

  • Role: Joint Engineer
  • Tools: Siemens NX, Autodesk Fusion 360, CNC machining, 3D printing, FEM
  • Duration: 1 year

Final design

The final design included eight motorized joints: four linear joints using planetary roller screws and four rotational joints using harmonic drives with belt transmission. The focus was on modularity, manufacturability, and serviceability.

Production

Frame and joint parts were largely produced in-house using CNC machining and conventional milling and turning. 3D printing was used for prototyping and covers.

  • Materials: 7075-T6 aluminium, AISI 304/316 stainless steel, titanium
  • Finish: black or transparent anodized
