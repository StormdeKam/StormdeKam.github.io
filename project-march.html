Project MARCH

Overview

Mechanical design of exoskeleton joints developed within a multidisciplinary team. Work focused on robust joint kinematics, lightweight structural design and manufacturable subassemblies under tight deadlines.

Summary

This project delivered a set of modular joint components for an assistive exoskeleton used in gait support. My role included CAD, tolerance analysis, rapid prototyping and verification testing. Key outcomes: reduced part count, improved joint stiffness-to-weight ratio, and simplified assembly procedure.

  • Role: Mechanical designer (CAD, prototyping)
  • Tools: SolidWorks, 3D printing, hand calculations
  • Duration: 6 months

CNC & Production

CNC'd parts were produced in-house for small-batch testing. Key learnings: tooling fixturing reduced setup time, and standard stock sizes minimized waste.

  • Material: 7075-T6 / aluminium (selected parts)
  • Finish: anodized or bead-blasted depending on function
  • Tolerances: +/- 0.1 mm where mating surfaces required
